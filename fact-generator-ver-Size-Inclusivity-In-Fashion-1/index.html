Size inclusivity is a company philosophy that involves producing an extended size range for every clothing line, employing models in a diverse size range, and, in general, affording everyone access to flattering clothing regardless of size. Though the majority of women wear sizes 14 to 18, many brands still consider this size range to be “plus-size”. This completely ignores the fat community, which requires sizing extending up to at least 26. In the future, companies should strive to cater to the needs of the fat community without forgetting customers who fall into the range of petite, junior, and small.
